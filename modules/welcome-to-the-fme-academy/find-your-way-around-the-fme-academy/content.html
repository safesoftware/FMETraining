Learning Objectives

After completing this lesson, you’ll be able to:

  • Navigate lessons, courses, and Learning Paths.
  • Explain how you earn points, badges, and ranks.

What’s a Learning Path?

A Learning Path is an ordered group of courses that provides a guided learning path to learning a new skill, product, or role.

A course covers a single learning topic. While Learning Paths are great for learning a comprehensive set of related topics, you can also mix and match courses freely. Say that you’re an FME user with five years of experience. It’s not worth your time to work through the Integrate Your Data with the FME Platform beginner Learning Path—you know most of that stuff already. Instead, you can pick and choose the courses that address the gaps in your knowledge or to help you grow your career in other ways.

A course is broken down into lessons. Each lesson covers a subtopic within a course and has a quiz at the end. When you ace the quiz, you get a healthy dose of points that add to the total displayed on your profile. When you’ve completed all of the lessons in a course, you earn a new badge.

When you earn points and badges, you advance ranks. When you begin you’re a Scout, and once you’ve earned your first badge you’re a Hiker. Eventually, you could even be an Adventurer (10 badges and 9,000 points).

What’s an Exercise?

Many lessons include an interactive FME exercise. A Resources section will provide you with links to interactive tutorials and starting workspaces when necessary.

Most FME Academy courses assume you have access to FME Desktop and FME Server. You can follow along with the step-by-step instructions using your own copy of FME. Many quizzes will ask questions that require you to work through the exercise.

If you need access to FME, you can:

  • Sign up for a free trial
  • Use a virtual machine to gain temporary access to FME
  • Ask your administrator for a license
  • Purchase FME

Mix Things Up with Trailmix

There is a lot of learning material available for FME, and it’s organized in different ways. That’s why we give you a way to group courses and other content together and share them on your own terms. Did you ever make a mixtape or a music playlist? A trailmix is like a mixtape, but of courses, Learning Paths, and other external content instead of songs. You can keep a trailmix to yourself, or share it with anyone. Use a trailmix to set your own goals, or use it to onboard new employees by giving them a list of content to work through.

To create a trailmix, hover over the Learn tab, click Trailmixes, then click Create a trailmix. Give your trailmix a name, description, and custom URL, then click Next. From there, you can add whatever content you wish. Click  on any course, Learning Path, project, or superbadge to add it to your trailmix. Once you’ve created a mix to your liking, share it with anyone you’d like by sending them the unique URL.

Find What You’re Looking for with Search and Filters

We want to make it easy to find what you’re looking for in the Academy. Use the search bar at the top of the screen to look for something specific, or you can filter courses and Learning Paths by feature area, product, role, or level. You can sort search results on the results page by clicking the Sort dropdown, which defaults to Most Relevant. If, for example, you're short on time, you can sort by quickest to complete. If you're getting close to that next rank, maybe you want to sort results by most points.

To filter, view the full list of courses and click a role, level, product, or tag at the top of the screen.


You can choose as many filters as you want. If you don’t see what you’re looking for and want to start over, click Clear filters.

Keep Track of Courses and Learning Paths with Favorites

Once you’ve found a course or Learning Path that fits your needs, you don’t want to waste time trying to locate it again. Click  at the bottom left of a Learning Path or course to favorite it. The next time you’re on the Academy, you’ll see the content you favorited at the top of the full list of courses or Learning Paths, respectively.

You can also see your favorites at any time by clicking your profile image in the top-right corner of Learning Pathhead and choosing Favorites.

Show Off What You Know

You've worked hard to earn points and badges, and your profile is the place to show them off. Click your profile image in the top-right corner of Learning Pathhead and click Profile to see all the badges and points you've earned so far, and a breakdown of skills you've learned.

Eventually we'll let you view (and share) your FME Community and FME Academy badges on your Safe Software profile.
